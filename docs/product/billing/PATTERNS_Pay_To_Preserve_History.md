# PATTERNS: Pay To Preserve History

## Why This Design Exists

The Blood Ledger monetizes through a unique **frictionless post-paid billing** model, contrasting traditional game monetization. The core principle is: **"The simulation is free. You pay to save your history."** This approach aligns with the game's emergent narrative nature, where the value lies in the player's unique story and the persistence of their choices.

## Executive Summary

*   **Monetization Model:** Frictionless post-paid billing – players register a credit card once, play freely, and receive a monthly invoice. This mirrors API billing.
*   **Unit of Charge:** **Per Moment saved.** A "Moment" is a piece of canon (e.g., dialogue, oath, secret, event). Players are minting permanent history, not renting CPU time.
*   **Target Behavior:** Players engage deeply with the game without constantly tracking costs, leading to organic exploration and discovery. The monthly invoice reflects the value of the story they've created.
*   **Revenue Distribution:** The model is "whale-focused," expecting 80% of revenue from 5% of players spending $500+/month.
*   **Pitch:** "~$3 per hour of story. Like a Pro DM, but available 24/7."

## Why This Model Fits Blood Ledger

### The Curiosity Engine
The game's design fosters continuous curiosity. Each interaction holds genuine value, prompting players to explore further, which directly translates to consumption and monetization without feeling transactional.

### No Pay-to-Win
Whales "discover more" and create deeper, richer histories rather than gaining competitive advantages. The quality of the simulation remains equal for all players, making spending an investment in narrative depth.

### Precedent: API Billing Psychology
This model leverages the psychological success of API billing (e.g., Anthropic, OpenAI), where users continuously derive value without friction and accept monthly invoices as a reflection of sustained utility.
