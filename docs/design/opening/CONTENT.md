## COMPANION REFLECTION TEMPLATE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

After the questions, Aldric reflects. This is where the LLM synthesizes.

**Structure:**

1. **Recognition** — "You're not what I expected"
2. **Mirroring** — Aldric finds himself in the player's words
3. **Offer** — Something concrete based on what was learned
4. **Seal** — The relationship crystallizes

**Example reflection (based on actual test):**

> "You're not what I expected."
>
> *He sets down the whetstone. Looks at you directly now.*
>
> "Most men with a grudge against Edmund — they want blood. Simple. I understand simple. But you..."
>
> *He shakes his head slowly.*
>
> "You want to build something. In *this* world."
>
> ---
>
> "I've been a sword for other men most of my life. Never asked why."
>
> *He turns back to you.*
>
> "But a tribe. Outcasts. People who don't fit..."
>
> *Something flickers in his expression — recognition.*
>
> "I've never fit either."
>
> ---
>
> "You said your thinking is different. That your words don't resonate."
>
> *He prods the fire.*
>
> "So here's what I can offer: I'll translate. When we're dealing with merchants and thegns — I'll be your bridge. You see the patterns. I'll find the words."
>
> *A nod, like sealing an agreement.*
>
> "Partners."
