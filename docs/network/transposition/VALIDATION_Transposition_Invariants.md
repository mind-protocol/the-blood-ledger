# VALIDATION: Transposition Invariants

## Invariants

This document defines the non-negotiable "Safety Locks" that ensure the absolute integrity and coherence of the player's "Local World" during the transposition of "Imported Entities." These invariants protect the player's immediate, tangible experience from being undermined by contradictory or nonsensical content generated by the "Bleed-Through" feature.

## 5. Algorithm: Safety Locks on Ground Truth

The following rules are absolute checks that ensure the integrity of the player's immediate, tangible experience. The goal is to add narrative richness and create "The world moved" moments, not to generate frustrating or nonsensical contradictions that break immersion.

### Lock 1: Prohibit Physical Override

*   **Invariant:** An imported entity can never be materialized at a `PLACE` where a Local World character has `Physical Presence` confirmed by the `Canon Holder`.
*   **Check:** Before integration, verify that the intended `PLACE` of the imported entity does not conflict with the established physical presence of any Local World character.
*   **Action on Violation:** If an import would cause two characters to occupy the same essential space in a contradictory manner, the import is **deferred, relocated, or rejected**.
*   **Purpose:** This lock directly protects the player's immediate sensory reality, ensuring that their current environment and character interactions remain believable.

### Lock 2: Defer to Local Canon

*   **Invariant:** If a conflict is so fundamental that it cannot be resolved by the cascade of Renaming, Relocation, and Fuzzing strategies, the Local World's canon takes absolute precedence.
*   **Check:** After all conflict resolution strategies have been attempted, verify that the modified imported entity is fully compliant with the Local World's "Ground Truth Canon."
*   **Action on Violation:** If full compliance cannot be achieved, the transposition algorithm will **fail gracefully**. The conflicting parts of the imported entity are **discarded**.
*   **Purpose:** This lock ensures that the coherence and integrity of the Local World are preserved above all else, even if it means sacrificing parts of the imported narrative. It prevents the introduction of unresolvable paradoxes.

## Overall Purpose

This layered logic ensures that while other worlds may bleed through, the player's world, and their story, remains uniquely their own, consistent, and impactful.
